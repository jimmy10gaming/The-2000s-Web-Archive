AFV Official Rules

AMERICA’S FUNNIEST HOME VIDEOS

OFFICIAL CONTEST RULES

SEASON XXIII 2012 – 2013

(Revised 2/26/13)


I.                GENERAL DESCRIPTION
 
Cara Communications Corporation (d/b/a Vin Di Bona Productions) is the sponsor of the Season XXIII contest (“Contest”) and the producer (“Producer”) of “America’s Funniest Home Videos,” (the “Program”), a television series featuring home videos seeks submission by members of the public of their home videos showing funny and/or unique events for possible telecast on the ABC Television Network owned by American Broadcasting Companies, Inc. (“Network” or “ABC”).  At Producer and/or Network’s sole discretion, videos may also be posted on ABC.com and may be further used as stated herein or in the submission materials.  Only videos conforming to Network’s Broadcast Standards and Practices, based on Producer’s and Network’s judgment, as they determine in their sole discretion, will be eligible for consideration in the Contest.
 
The Contest will consist of approximately eighteen (18) Contestant shows, three (3) One Hundred Thousand Dollar prize shows, and one (1) Grand Prize award show in the 2012 – 2013 season.  In each Contestant show, there will be a first, second and third prize awarded for best videos.  The 18 Contestant shows will be divided into 3 blocks. Each block will consist of between 5 and 7 Contestant shows, and will be followed by a One Hundred Thousand Dollar prize show, which will feature the first place winners from each of the Contestant shows in the corresponding block of Contestant shows.  The Grand Prize award show at the end of the season will feature a competition between the three (3) One Hundred Thousand Dollar prize show winners from the 2012 – 2013 season.  There will be one (1) prize awarded for the best video in the Grand Prize show. The number of videos per show and/or shows per block are subject to change in Producer’s sole discretion.  If the 2012 – 2013 season series is unable to be completed due to forces beyond Producer’s control, such as cancellation or a guild strike, Producer reserves the right not to award any or all of the prizes set forth above. Please see section VII of these rules regarding additional rules for awarding of prizes.
 
II.               PROCEDURES
 
1.    Persons wishing to enter may send as many different home videos as they wish to “America’s Funniest Home Videos,” P.O. Box 4333, Hollywood, CA 90078, or submit home videos on the AFV site at www.AFV.com by following the instructions on the website or via the AFV mobile application for iPhone and iPod Touch available for free download on the iTunes App Store. Videos will be reviewed on an ongoing basis.  See section VIII for information on deadlines.
 
2.    If submitting by mail, Entrants should include their return address, email address, if available, telephone number and a brief written description of the videos being submitted.
 
3.    All submissions become the sole property of Producer and may be used in any manner, commercial or otherwise, in any and all media and/or technologies now known or hereafter devised throughout the universe in perpetuity.  Entrants submitting by mail should include a written request and a $3.00 handling fee per video, in U.S. money order, if they wish Producer to return their video(s), which will be done on a rolling basis during the 2012– 2013 season.  Producer reserves the right to destroy all videos for which a written request for return and the handling fee were not sent at the time of submission(s).  Producer is not responsible for lost, damaged or misdirected videos under any circumstances.  Please keep a copy of your submission.
 
4.    If you are mailing your submission, all home video formats are acceptable (VHS, 8MM, Hi-8, digital video).  Home videos shot for commercial purposes are not eligible for any prizes.  Home videos transferred to DVD or CD-ROM will be accepted provided they meet eligibility and broadcast requirements.  8MM film transferred to videotape is also accepted.  Digital chips will not be accepted.
 
5.    AFV is broadcast in High Definition (HD) so, if submitting videos via the AFV.com website, please submit your highest quality video file up to 600MB.  The recommended specifications are 30 Frames Per Second with 1280×720 Resolution. Acceptable file formats are: .WMV, .AVI, .MOV and .MPG.  Producer particularly recommends MPEG4 (MPG) compression types (CODECS) such as H.264, DivX, Xvid, and AVC. If using the AFV mobile app to submit videos to AFV.com, the length and file size limit, per submission, is up to 20 minutes and 300 MBs, respectively, over a Wi-Fi connection, or, up to 10 minutes and 10 MBs, respectively, over a cellular network. Message & data rates may apply for videos submitted via the AFV mobile app; Entrants are responsible for all fees incurred through their wireless device associated with participation in this Contest.  Please consult your wireless service provider for details regarding your message & data plan(s).  Instructions for uploading the videos as well as downloading the consent forms and/or other releases as requested by Producer (collectively, the “Releases”) can be found on the America’s Funniest Home Videos home page at: www.AFV.com.  Entrant understands and agrees his/her video submission may receive exposure on AFV.com and/or ABC.com and may be subjected to comments and popularity ratings from the public at large.  Producer is not responsible in any manner for any critical or derogatory remarks posted by the public pertaining to an Entrant or an Entrant’s video. Additional information regarding online submissions may be available at AFV.com.
 
6.    Videos that purport to be spontaneous must be so, or Entrant must explicitly disclose the circumstances of the taping at time of entry.
 
7.    Producer reserves the right to edit and/or alter any submission in Producer’s sole discretion.
 
8.    Producer’s decisions regarding all Contest matters are final and non-appealable.  All submissions are subject to the terms of these rules and all Entrants agree to be bound by these rules and Producer’s decisions.  Producer reserves the right in its sole discretion to amend these rules at any time and Producer will post the revised rules on AFV.com. Due to the ongoing nature of the contest, the rules are updated several times throughout the season; please review them on AFV.com frequently.  The rules in effect at whatever time the submission is judged will apply regardless of submission date.
 
9.    Producer reserves the right to alter the number of contestants in each show and the number of Contestant shows in each block at any time.
 
10. IV.             JUDGING CRITERIA
 
1.    Videos will be judged for inclusion in shows and for prizes on the basis of their humor and/or uniqueness.  Examples of videos that have been considered include those showing silly blunders at social occasions; unexpected foul-ups involving children; animal antics; slip-ups during sports or vacation activities; bloopers during plays, recitals, parades or speeches; practical jokes; natural phenomena; and life’s annoyances.   From time to time, the Producer may request home videos on specific topics, such as for the show’s “Assignment America” requests.  Such submissions will be included with the general submission pool for Contest purposes and will compete with the general submission pool for any prizes unless otherwise stated.  All videos must conform to Network’s Broadcast Standards and Practices policies.
 
2.    Producer will select videos for inclusion in each Contestant show as follows:
 
A.    Each video will be assigned a number, and logged in.
 
B.    Producer’s representatives will conduct an initial review (the “Initial Review”) of all submissions and will (as determined by Producer in its sole discretion) advance those videos reflecting a sufficient level of humor and/or uniqueness to warrant further consideration.  Any video which does not advance past Initial Review will be rejected for use as a contestant video at that time. Producer reserves the right to reevaluate any submission at any time, but has no obligation to do so.
 
C.    Producer will view videos that are advanced beyond Initial Review for possible consideration in a particular show.
 
V.              CONTESTANT SHOWS ($10,000)
 
1.    The voting procedure for the Contestant shows will be as follows, unless otherwise specified:
 
A.    Of the videos included in each Contestant show, the Producer will select 3 finalists based on humor and/or uniqueness.  During the taping of the show, members of the studio audience will vote for their favorite video from among the 3 finalists and use an electronic voting system (or an alternate voting system, including but not limited to voting by the general public in person, by telephone or over the internet, as determined by Producer).  The first, second and third prizes will be awarded based on the number of valid votes cast for each of the 3 finalists.
 
B.    In the event of a tie for the $10,000 prize, the vote will be taken again, between the tying entries, until the tie is broken.  In the event of a tie for second place, both videos will receive the second place prize money (and there will be no third prize awarded).
 
C.    Producer will pre-screen audience members and obtain signed affidavits in which audience members attest (i) that they are not related to or acquainted with, in fact or by reputation, any of the individuals who submitted, shot or appear in any of the finalist videos and (ii) that they have no relationship to the Producer, the AFV contestants, ABC, The Walt Disney Company or any parent, subsidiary and/or affiliated company of any of the foregoing entities or any employee of any of the foregoing entities.  Audience members with any relationship to AFV contestants and audience members who are employees, representatives or agents of Cara Communications Corporation, d/b/a Vin Di Bona Productions, ABC, The Walt Disney Company or the parent, subsidiary, and/or any affiliated companies of any of the foregoing and anyone involved in the production, administration or judging of the Contest, as well as immediate family/same household members of anyone so employed or engaged are ineligible to vote.
 
2.    Producer reserves the right to use alternate judging and/or voting procedures, as determined by Producer, for any show or segment including, without limitation, for any Contestant show, special shows or segments, in Producer’s sole discretion.
 
3.    For the Contestant shows, the first prize for each Contestant show is $10,000.  Second prize is $3,000.  Third prize is $2,000.
 
4.    Videos included in a Contestant show and telecast are not eligible for first, second or third place prize in future Contestant shows.  At Producer’s sole option, any video may be retained and considered for use and reuse, and used and reused, in any subsequent edition of the Program, any other program or production, and/or for ancillary or other use in any and all media now known or hereafter devised throughout the universe in perpetuity.
 
5.    Contestants may not post their video on any social media sites during the entire contest season, and thereafter, only with private viewing settings on YouTube only.
 
VI.             $100,000 SHOWS AND GRAND PRIZE SHOW:
 
1.    The season’s Contestant shows will be divided into 3 blocks as set forth above, at Producer’s sole discretion.  The first place winners from each block will compete for a prize of $100,000.   If the Program and/or any episodes are cancelled or cannot be completed prior to the completion of all Contestant shows in a block, Producer reserves the right not to award a $100,000 prize and/or the Grand Prize, to reduce or substitute for the announced prize, and/or to modify the means by which a winner will be selected.
 
2.    The 3 winners of the $100,000 shows will compete for a vacation prize in  the Grand Prize show.  Prize details for the Grand Prize are attached to these rules (see Attachment A) and are subject to change.
 
3.    The Grand Prize consists of a vacation to the Walt Disney World® Resort in Florida and a choice of an Adventures by Disney vacation as specified in Attachment A.  The approximate retail value of the prize is between $37,196.77 and $60,036.77 depending on which Adventures by Disney vacation the winner selects. (See Attachment A to these rules for trip details, conditions and restrictions.)  Prize details are subject to change.
 
4.    The voting procedure for the $100,000 shows and the Grand Prize show will be as follows, unless otherwise specified:
 
A)   The videos will be screened for members of the public at a venue, date(s) and time(s) to be determined by Producer.  The videos will be shown in random order without added voice-over or effects.  The video receiving the greatest number of valid votes will be deemed the winner.
 
B)   Producer will pre-screen audience members and obtain signed affidavits in which audience members attest (i) that they are not related to or acquainted with, in fact or by reputation, any of the individuals who submitted, shot or appear in any of the finalist videos and (ii) that they have no relationship to the Producer, the AFV contestants, ABC, The Walt Disney Company or any parent, subsidiary and/or affiliated company of any of the foregoing or to any employee of any of the foregoing.  Audience members with any relationship to AFV contestants and audience members who are employees, representatives or agents of Cara Communications Corporation, d/b/a Vin Di Bona Productions, ABC, The Walt Disney Company or the parent, subsidiary, and/or any affiliated companies of any of the foregoing and anyone involved in the production, administration or judging of the Contest, as well as immediate family/same household members of anyone so employed or engaged are ineligible to vote.
 
5.    The criteria for selecting the prize winner of each block will be humor and/or uniqueness.
 
6.    In the event of a tie, the studio audience at the taping of the $100,000 shows and/or the Grand Prize show will vote between the tying entries, until the tie is broken.
 
7.    Producer reserves the right to use alternate judging or voting procedures, as determined by the Producer, for the $100,000 shows and/or the Grand Prize show as Producer may determine in its sole discretion.

1.    Prizes will be awarded to the person(s) submitting the video up to 90 days after initial telecast, provided they certify and establish to Producer’s satisfaction that they are the owner(s) of the video and further provided that they have complied with the official rules and signed all necessary Releases and any other forms as determined by Producer.
 
2.    Winners of any prize are solely responsible for all federal, state and local taxes.
 
3.    If the anticipated complement of episodes for the 2012 – 2013 season series is not completed for any reason, including without limitation as a result of program cancellation or labor dispute, Producer reserves the right to terminate, suspend and/or modify the competition, award the prizes by any other means Producer deems appropriate, and/or limit, reduce or not award the announced prizes.  Thus, for example, if for any reason the anticipated complement of episodes of qualifying Contestant Shows has not been completed in any block of shows, Producer reserves the right in its sole discretion to not produce the $100,000 Show and not award the $100,000 prize for that block of Contestant Shows.  If there are fewer than three (3) qualifying $100,000 award winners, then Producer in its sole discretion may decide not to produce a Grand Prize show and not award the Grand Prize, or to award the Grand Prize via alternate means.  In all instances, in order for the applicable prize to be awarded, either for a Contestant show, or a $100,000 show or a Grand Prize show, such episode must be produced and telecast.
 
4.    A prize of one (1) AFV tee-shirt shall be awarded to an Entrant if their video is broadcast for the first time on an AFV episode during the 2012 – 2013 season.  Only one (1) tee-shirt per video is allotted.  If mailing address and/or contact information for the Entrant is no longer valid, Producer is not responsible for delivery of the shirt.
 
VIII.           CONTEST DEADLINES
 
1.    The deadline date for the first Contest block of the 2012 – 2013 season is October 4, 2012.  The deadline date for the second Contest block is December 7, 2012. The deadline date for the third Contest block is March 8, 2013. All videos must be received by Producer on or before the deadline dates to be eligible for inclusion in that block.
 
Deadlines are subject to change at Producer’s sole discretion.
 
IX.             RELEASES
 
1.    All Entrants must sign all general Releases and other documents in the form provided by Producer or be eliminated from consideration and declared ineligible to win any prize.  Releases will include, among other things, a statement that the videos were the exclusive property of the Entrant(s) at the time they were submitted and that the events purported to be spontaneous were in fact spontaneous and not staged.
 
Releases must be obtained from all identifiable persons depicted, seen or heard in the videos.  A parent or guardian must sign the Release forms for minors.
